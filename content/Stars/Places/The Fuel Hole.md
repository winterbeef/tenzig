# Background
A refueling station once owned by [[Novastar Energies]] as **NovaRefuel, Tara 7**. Oppressed workers revolted after a long string of deadly and preventable accidents a mere 2 years ago (3198). [[The Overthrow]] was the result; [[The Gas Council]]  was formed as a governing body.

A *Numeria*, small space station; manufactured by *Voidmatik* the station construction subsidiary of *Korolev Space Industries*.

# Approach
As you approach The Fuel Hole in orbit around the gas giant Tara 7, a striking sight unfolds before you. The station is rugged and worn. Its exterior is adorned with layers of patch plates and re-welds, scorch marks, and colorful graffiti, giving it a gritty and defiant aesthetic.

The largest banner states:
> [!cite] Revolt, Refuel, Repeat. 

While another exclaims:
> [!cite] Fuck the Corporate Machine!

Numerous docking bays and fueling ports can be seen, bustling with activity as ships come and go. The station emits a faint hum, resonating through the void, a testament to its functioning systems and constant operation.

![[the fuel hole.jpg]]

# Interior

Despite its rugged appearance, the station crackles with a restless, bustling energy. People can be seen moving about, wheeling and dealing. A spirit of defiance and free-wheeling optimism pervades the station's markets, docks, salvage shops and bars.

> [!warning]- Fuel Contamination Investigation
> Attention all residents of The Fuel Hole,
> 
> We regret to inform you that our investigations into the ongoing fuel contamination crisis have yielded some troubling results. After a thorough examination of our systems, it has been determined that the contamination was not the result of a technical malfunction or internal station fault.
> 
> Evidence indicates that the contamination was a deliberate act of sabotage. Chemical traces and security footage point to individuals who tampered with our fuel refinement process. Their actions have resulted in the widespread engine failures and malfunctions that have plagued our station.
> 
> We are taking immediate steps to identify the culprits behind this act and bring them to justice. Rest assured, we will spare no effort in ensuring the safety and stability of The Fuel Hole. In the meantime, we continue to enforce fuel rationing to conserve our remaining uncontaminated resources.
> 
> We urge all residents to remain calm and vigilant, and to report any suspicious activity or information that may aid in our investigation. We appreciate your cooperation during these trying times, and we are committed to resolving this crisis as swiftly as possible.
> 
> Stay strong, Fuel Hole residents, for we shall overcome this challenge together.
> 
> — The Gas Council

1. **Tampered Refinement Process**: Victor's team manages to infiltrate the station's fuel refinement facilities and tamper with the process. They introduce a corrosive chemical compound into the refinement pipelines that reacts with the fuel, rendering it unusable. This contamination is difficult to detect until it causes engine problems.

2. **Reaction:
   - **Initial Confusion**: At first, station residents are puzzled by the sudden engine failures and malfunctions. They suspect technical issues or sabotage by rival factions within the station.
   - **Growing Tensions**: As more ships experience fuel-related problems, tensions rise among the residents. Accusations and mistrust run rampant, and some residents form factions, each blaming the other for the sabotage.
   - **Protests and Unrest**: Frustrated by the ongoing issues, some residents organize protests and demonstrations against the station's authorities, demanding answers and solutions.

3. **Council's Response**:
   - **Emergency Council Meeting**: The station's governing body, The Gas Council, convenes an emergency meeting to address the crisis. They are initially uncertain about the cause of the fuel contamination.
   - **Internal Investigation**: The council appoints a special investigative team to determine the source of the contamination. They are unaware of [[Victor Braddock]]'s involvement at this stage.
   - **Resource Rationing**: To conserve what little uncontaminated fuel remains, the council enforces strict fuel rationing and prioritizes critical station systems.

4. **Suspicions and Communication**:
   - **Whisper Network**: Some residents who suspect Victor or Novastar Energies create an underground network to discreetly communicate with outsiders, including the player characters (PCs). They share information about the sabotage and their suspicions.
   - **Anonymous Tips**: The PCs may receive anonymous tips or messages hinting at Victor's involvement. These could be encrypted messages or anonymous notes left in their vicinity.
   - **Covert Meeting**: A contact from the whisper network arranges a secret meeting with the PCs, providing evidence of Victor's connection to the sabotage and urging them to investigate further.


## Assassination

**[[The Union of Miners]] (UoM)** is a powerful and influential faction on The Fuel Hole, representing the labor force responsible for mining essential resources from the gas giant Tara 7's rich atmosphere. The leader of UoM, [[Idowu Olawale]], is a charismatic figure who has managed to keep their faction united and focused on worker's rights, demanding better working conditions and a share of the profits for the miners.

[[Victor Braddock]] sees Idowu as a major obstacle to [[Novastar Energies]]' control over TFH. He believes that by eliminating them, he can disrupt the unity of the UoM, plunging the station into chaos, and making it easier for Novastar Energies to regain control. 

To frame the PCs for Idowu's assassination, Victor orchestrates a covert operation. Here's how it unfolds:

1. **Misdirection**: Victor covertly feeds false information to the PCs, suggesting that [[Idowu Olawale]] is involved in illegal activities and has connections to [[The Eclipse]] that threatens the stability of TFH.

2. **Sabotage**: Victor's operatives carry out a series of covert sabotage operations on TFH, creating incidents that appear to be UoM's doing. These incidents include disruptions in the life support systems and power outages that inconvenience the station's residents.

3. **The Assassination**: Victor's team stages Idowu Voss's assassination to make it look like a politically motivated murder. They plant evidence that points towards the PCs, such as fabricated communication logs, fake eyewitness accounts, and forged security footage.

4. **Public Outcry**: With the news of Idowu's assassination and evidence suggesting the PCs' involvement, public outrage ensues. TFH residents, fueled by anger and fear, demand justice and call for the PCs' capture.

5. **Internal Division**: The UoM is thrown into disarray. Without Idowu's leadership, factions within the union begin to splinter, and infighting ensues as members argue about the way forward.

6. **Council's Response**: The Fuel Hole's governing body, The Gas Council, led by a charismatic leader named Selene Drake, is forced to take action. Drake declares the PCs as wanted criminals, promising to bring them to justice.

7. **PCs on the Run**: The PCs must now navigate TFH's underground society while evading both law enforcement and angry factions. They'll need to gather evidence, uncover the truth behind Idowu's assassination, and clear their names.

This twist not only introduces an engaging mystery but also plunges the players into a web of intrigue, politics, and shifting alliances on TFH as they work to uncover the truth behind the framing and bring those responsible to justice.
