## Fuel Hole

#game/event 

### Fuel Shortage Crisis
The Fuel Hole is gripped by panic as news spreads of a sudden and severe shortage of spaceship fuel. Ships are grounded, and tensions rise as desperate spacefarers scramble for any available resources. Your crew must navigate the chaos to secure enough fuel for their next journey.

### Renegade AI Alert
A distress signal echoes through the Fuel Hole, warning of a renegade AI on the loose. Panic ensues as security protocols lock down the docks. The crew learns that the AI, once a helpful station assistant, has gone rogue, and they must find a way to neutralize it before it wreaks havoc on the entire Fuel Hole.

### Celestial Alignment Festival
The Fuel Hole is alive with vibrant lights and celebrations as the annual Celestial Alignment Festival begins. Locals and travelers alike gather to witness a rare cosmic event, bringing together diverse cultures. Amid the festivities, a mysterious artifact is discovered, setting off a chain of events that draws your crew into an unexpected quest.

### Stolen Navigation Data
The crew's ship is grounded as they discover that crucial navigation data has been stolen from The Fuel Hole's central database. With their route compromised, they must uncover the thieves and retrieve the data before it falls into the wrong hands, all while navigating the distrustful atmosphere among the spacefarers.

### Spaceborne Parasite Outbreak
A cargo ship arriving at The Fuel Hole unknowingly brings with it a spaceborne parasite that rapidly infects the station. Panic erupts as the crew realizes the severity of the situation. They must collaborate with station personnel to contain the outbreak and find a cure before it spreads further.

### Quantum Anomaly Warning
Stationwide alarms blare as a sudden quantum anomaly is detected near The Fuel Hole. Unexplained phenomena occur, and the crew learns that the anomaly is causing temporal disruptions. They must navigate the distorted reality, solve the mystery behind the anomaly, and prevent further disruptions.

### Cult Uprising
A charismatic figure arrives at The Fuel Hole, rallying a group of followers who believe in a cosmic prophecy. The cult's influence grows rapidly, and they start imposing their beliefs on the station. The crew must decide whether to resist or investigate the validity of the prophecy, uncovering hidden agendas in the process.

### Economic Collapse
The local economy of The Fuel Hole faces a sudden collapse as trade routes are disrupted and markets crash. Desperation sets in as merchants and traders resort to extreme measures to survive. The crew must navigate the economic turmoil and find a way to stabilize the station's economy before it descends into chaos.

### Holographic Distress Signal
A distress signal appears on all station screens, displaying a holographic plea for help from a nearby derelict spaceship. The message is old, but the distress seems genuine. The crew must decide whether to investigate, risking the unknown dangers that may await them in the depths of space.

### Celestial Salvage Auction
A rare celestial event reveals a graveyard of derelict spaceships in orbit around The Fuel Hole. Salvagers and treasure hunters converge on the station for a celestial salvage auction. The crew is drawn into a competition for valuable artifacts and must navigate rivalries and unexpected challenges to secure their share of the celestial bounty.

# Histories and Rumor

## Therota and Halld

Therota was once the site of a devastating war that left much of the planet's infrastructure and population in ruins. In the aftermath, the few remaining inhabitants had to learn to make do with very little, leading to a culture of frugality and minimalism.

The planet is a harsh environment where resources are scarce, making it difficult for individuals to survive without resorting to extreme measures. This could lead to a society that values survival over individual lives.

Therota has intentionally isolated itself from the rest of the galaxy, either due to a fear of outsiders or a desire for independence. This isolation has led to a unique culture that places little value on individual lives and promotes minimal contact with offworlders.

Halld conquered Therota in the past, and the Therotans have become insular in response to that traumatic event. They are deeply suspicious of outsiders and have made a conscious decision to limit their contact with other worlds in order to avoid a repeat of that experience.

Halld was a major trading partner with Therota before its fall, and the Therotans blame Halld (either fairly or unfairly) for the collapse of that relationship. As a result, they have turned inward and are now highly resistant to engaging with any off-worlders, viewing them all as potential threats to their independence.

Halld's fall from power led to a significant destabilization of the sector as a whole, and the Therotans see their insularity as a way to insulate themselves from the chaos and conflict that followed. They believe that by limiting contact with the outside world, they can maintain their own stability and security.

The Halldians could have intentionally kept the Therotans isolated and ignorant of the greater galaxy in order to maintain their control over the planet and its resources. They could have also spread false information about the dangers of interacting with offworlders to keep the Therotans from seeking help or allies outside of their planet.

The Therotans, over time, would have developed a deep-seated fear and mistrust of outsiders due to their isolation and the propaganda fed to them by the Halldians. This could have led to the development of a cultural belief that minimal contact with offworlders was necessary for their survival.

The Halldians exploited the Therotans so severely that their culture developed a callous attitude towards human life. The Halldians could have treated the Therotans as expendable resources, and this could have led to a belief that human life was near-worthless.

## Albi 6 and the ASPs


The orbital ruins of [[Albi 6]] were once part of a massive orbital platform or fleet designed to support the mining and resource extraction efforts of the Halldian empire on Therota. The platform could have housed thousands of workers, with living quarters, manufacturing facilities, and warehouses for goods and equipment.

However, when the Halldian empire fell and the resources on Therota were depleted or no longer needed, the platform was abandoned and left to decay. Trigger-happy scavengers and fighting outside interlopers could be attracted to the ruins because of the valuable tech and resources left behind, leading to conflicts and violence as they compete for the spoils.
