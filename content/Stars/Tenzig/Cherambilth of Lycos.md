- **Toxic Garden:** An overgrown greenhouse filled with psi-mutated plants (Cherambilth contamination). One attacks by inducing hallucinations via direct neural resonance.
   
- **Relic Room:** A chamber of antique psychic tech, carefully maintained but forbidden. Luddite youth sneak in here for "communion” — a chance to see the truth of tech. Could be an RP or skill challenge.
   
- **EMP Dead Zone:** Tech shuts down, and psychics get sensory feedback loops. Navigating it requires a combination of Exert, Survive, and psi control.
   

- **Know (Pretech):** Decode old symbols on a console that controls the Cherambilth containment lattice.
- **Program:** Create a feedback loop in a psi-sensor array to mask the team's psychic signatures.
- **Talk/Lead:** Convince a wavering luddite youth that psychic tech isn’t inherently evil.
- **Sneak:** Avoid drones co-opted by the luddites and stripped down to non-digital, analog guard units.


The cache is not inert — the Cherambilth here is semi-conscious, or at least resonant. It _remembers_ its use. When a psychic like Joe Chip approaches, it reacts. It might even try to communicate, leaking memories of experiments, dreams of a broken lab… or a map.



## **Room 1: Threshold of Reflection**

**Purpose:** Guardian / First Challenge  
**Concept:** The Preceptor Interface awaits. Glyphs flicker. Sensory qualia hum. This is not a terminal — it’s a gate of judgment.

**Encounter:**

- Trigger the **3-part psychic scan** (see previous message).
    
- Joe Chip may receive a unique inner vision of his own past.
    
- PCs failing the test may have to prove worth through **an alternate route** or service quest.
    

**Skills:** `Program`, `Connect`, `Lead`, `WIS Save`, `CHA Save`

**Twist:** A psionic echo forms a simulacrum of the PCs’ younger selves, asking them: _“What would you tell me, knowing what you know now?”_

## **Room 2: The Chamber of Contingency**

**Purpose:** Puzzle or Roleplaying  
**Concept:** Success with the Interface opens a vault, but inside is not information — it’s a **cognitive construct**. A mind-map of a lost Inuar dissenter: the [[Promiton]].

**Challenge:**

- Navigate the branching logic of its fragmented memory.
    
- Solve a puzzle involving **sequence alignment**, interpreting **broken knowledge-languages**, or reconstructing an **ethical timeline**.
    

**Skills:** `Know`, `Exert` (if psychic fatigue sets in), `Culture/Pretech`

**Outcome:** The PCs learn of the Cherambilth cache — and the warning encoded in the words:

> _“They fear because they remember. You must forget in order to pass.”_

---

## **Room 3: The Corridor of the Unseen**

**Purpose:** Trick or Setback  
**Concept:** En route to the Cherambilth coordinates, the party enters a tunnel cut into Lycos' rock-crust. The passage is zero-g.  
But **someone is already here** — watchers from the **Luddite enclave** have triggered anti-tech countermeasures.

**Encounter:**

- Hidden sonic or psychic dampeners mess with tech (glitches, hallucinations).
    
- PCs may be attacked by **Psionic Nulls** in stealth armor.
    
- Joe Chip may need to burn Effort to suppress overwhelming feedback.
    

**Skills:** `Sneak`, `Notice`, `Exert`, `Fix` (for shorting fields)

**Twist:** A captured Luddite, left behind by their comrades, begs for help. If they help him, he will tell them **how to pass the Final Gate**.

---

## **Room 4: Vault of Divergence**

**Purpose:** Climax or Conflict  
**Concept:** A vault — smooth, unmarked, impossible to interface with directly. Inside: a motherlode of Cherambilth... **and** fragments of Inuar code that _evolve_ when touched by psychic minds.

**Encounter:**

- The vault reacts uniquely to each psychic. One will **see a loved one**, one will **see their death**, one will **see the Archive burn**.
    
- Extracting the Cherambilth will **awaken a dormant defense**: a crystalline Inuar shell, powered by stored psi-energy — part guardian, part judge.
    

**Combat:** Inuar Guardian (AC 17, HP 30, Psi Shock aura)

**Skills:** `Shoot`, `Punch`, `Lead`, `Heal`, `Know` (to talk it down)

---

## **Room 5: Archive Echo**

**Purpose:** Revelation / Twist  
**Concept:** Surviving the vault grants more than treasure. The Preceptor System opens again, revealing that the **Luddites were once guided by the Archive**, but broke away after one of their own triggered a psychic collapse.

**Revelation:**

- The Archive is not merely neutral — it has a **plan**, and **may ask the PCs to help re-activate it** across Lycos.
    
- Joe Chip receives a direct neural echo: "You fled exploitation, but will you flee responsibility?”
    

**Hook Forward:** A massive, buried second core lies under Luddite territory, dormant.  
The Archive needs it — but the Luddites **may destroy it** if the PCs approach.
