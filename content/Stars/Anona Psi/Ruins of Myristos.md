# Legends

A pre-Scream city 
Rumors hint at pre-tech, items of historical significance, and other rarities hidden among the ruins of this city.

PCs might be hired by eccentric aristocracy ([[Octavius Cromwell]]) or curious scholars ([[Professor Aeneas Xanthopoulos]]).

But the map/key/bypass is available in [[Hierápolis]], and the [[Citizens of Faultless Virtue]] are suspicious of off-worlders.

[[Justinian Theodora]], salvage expert-at-large on Thora, has managed to liberate a few artifacts from the [[Ruins of Myristos#Spire Room|Spire Room]] of the Regius Eternus

# Regius Eternus

The citadel in the center of the [[Ruins of Myristos]]. Appears as a frustum upon frustum in the center of the ruins.  Roads radiate from the ring road that circumscribes the citadel, 50 meter diameter base

The profile of the tower seems an asymptotic curve, the tower becoming impossibly thin after it has risen a few dozen meters into the sky.

A 3m wide dais is present just inside the ring road. 

## Dais Entrance

**Scenario:** Musical notes on the dais need to be played in a specific sequence to access the citadel

**Clue:** carved into a short column in the dais are clues to the musical notation.  Some knowledge check (8+) may add a bonus the the next checks.

Each successful **Perform** or **Know** check plays a note correctly and advances the clock. A failed check adds to the Pursuers clock.

Musical Sequence clock
- [ ] 1st note
- [ ] 2nd
- [ ] 3rd
- [ ] 4th
- [ ] 5th
- [ ] OPEN

Pursuers clock
- [ ] 2 round the corner and take up positions (half cover is -2)
- [ ] A zap from the dais: physical save or 1d3 damage
- [ ] two attack from cover; 2 more arrive (4 total)
- [ ] 4 attacks
- [ ] A zap from the dais: mental save or 1d3 damage
- [ ] the leader arrives, granting +2 morale

## The Echoing Hall

This octagonal chamber features shifting mirrors and reflective surfaces.  there are 4 pillars holding up an arched ceiling, about 10 meters above.

If the room is searched a rotted body is slumped behind a pillar, written in blood "NO HELP".

As the room is entered, a hologram flickers into being.  The source is unknown.

## Spire Room

A towering chamber dominated by a central spire that reaches up into darkness. The spire is covered in strange, shifting glyphs that seem to rearrange themselves when looked at directly. It hums with a faint, rhythmic pulse.

A linguist may with study determine that the glyphs are related to an old language -- perhaps "Old Mandate".

## The War Room

A command center with holographic displays showing a three-dimensional map of the sector as it was during the height of the Mandate glory. The table in the center still projects the movements of long-dead fleets, and strange symbols blink urgently as if the war never ended.


# Room Ideas

### **Obsidian Chamber**
A vast, echoing room with smooth, black walls that absorb all light. The floor is engraved with indecipherable runes that pulse faintly when approached, and a feeling of dread fills the air. In the center, an altar-like structure hums with a forgotten power.

### **Hall of Echoes**
A long, narrow hallway where every sound is amplified and distorted. Strange, alien whispers seem to come from the walls, but the origin is unclear. The walls are lined with cracked, holographic panels displaying ghostly images of the past.

### **The Latticework Room**
A massive chamber filled with a complex web of crystalline structures suspended from the ceiling. The lattices emit a soft, musical tone as they sway, and hidden within them are fragile orbs that seem to contain swirling galaxies.

### **The Petrified Garden**
A vast room overgrown with alien flora that has turned to stone over the eons. The petrified plants glow faintly, casting eerie shadows. The center of the room is dominated by a massive tree-like structure, its roots penetrating deep into the floor.

### **The Memory Vault**
Rows of strange, obelisk-like devices line the walls, each filled with the recorded memories of the long-dead alien inhabitants. Interfacing with one might provide visions of their final days, but at the risk of mental overload.

### **Gravity Well Chamber**
A spherical room with no floor or ceiling, where gravity is completely distorted. The walls are lined with mysterious machinery, and small objects float in random directions, including fragments of alien technology and bones.

### **The Pool of Dreams**
A circular room with a central pool filled with an iridescent liquid. Gazing into the pool reveals visions of alien worlds and civilizations. However, those who stare too long might lose themselves in the dreams of the dead.

### **The Spire Room**
A towering chamber dominated by a central spire that reaches up into darkness. The spire is covered in strange, shifting glyphs that seem to rearrange themselves when looked at directly. It hums with a faint, rhythmic pulse.

### **The Time Capsule**
A room filled with rows of strange, egg-shaped pods, each containing a perfectly preserved alien figure. The room is cold and sterile, with frost covering every surface. Some pods are cracked or damaged, their contents missing.

### **The Cryo-Forge**
A vast, workshop-like area with ancient, alien machinery designed to manipulate matter at a molecular level. The room is freezing cold, and the remains of failed experiments litter the floor—twisted, half-formed creatures and technology.

### **Chamber of the Living Tapestry**
A long hall with walls covered in a thick, organic material that seems to be alive. The material shifts and pulses, forming strange images and scenes that tell the history of the aliens in a way that is both beautiful and unsettling.

### **The War Room**
A command center with holographic displays showing a three-dimensional map of the galaxy as it was during the alien’s reign. The table in the center still projects the movements of long-dead fleets, and strange symbols blink urgently as if the war never ended.
