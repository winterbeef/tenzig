# Quantum Dilemma

## Overview

A powerful quantum AI core is contained within a rigged container, armed with a small thermonuclear weapon set to detonate if opened improperly. Different factions vie for control, each with conflicting motives.

## Factions

### Faction A
- Avoids conflict for public relations.
- Well-equipped and wealthy.
- For profit.
- Unscrupulous owner willing to resort to murder.

### Faction B
- Values human life highly.
- Will sacrifice a few for the greater good.
- Wants to use the AI against Faction A.
- Knows Faction A is evil but lacks proof.
- Will destroy the container to prevent theft.
- Less well-funded than Faction A.

### Faction C
- Aims to destroy the container at any cost.
- Will try to convince the PC team to assist.
- Less well-funded than Faction B.
- Views the AI as a threat to humanity.
- Operates as a shadow organization with a mercenary front.

### Faction D
- A rich, eccentric, skilled thief.
- Has pretech anti-grav harness to reduce container size.
- Aims to avoid Faction C's goal of destroying the AI.
- Will attempt to steal the container during transport.

## The Container

- Dimensions: 75 cm x 60 cm x 45 cm, 60 kgs.
- Contains a quantum AI core and a small thermonuclear weapon.
- Can only be opened safely with the proper key/code/password/method.
- Detonation radius: 100m.

## Key Plot Element

The container's contents are a secret. Multiple challenging skill checks are required for the PCs to uncover the truth. Failed checks result in references to the container as something akin to Pandora's Box.

## Likely Outcomes

1. PC team retrieves the container successfully for Faction A, revealing their evil nature.
2. PC team is captured/defeated by Faction B, leading them to help expose Faction A.
3. Faction C succeeds in destroying the container (failure state) or convinces the PC team to assist.
4. Faction D steals the container and escapes. The PC team must track and steal it back, Mission Impossible style.
