Arachnoid. Tribal hunters of ~1000 persons in a tribe.

Eggs are laid in a *[[szaichib rikhool sacred nest.png|Rikhool]]* , a communal cradle.  The ritual of *Turhool'a* is the surrender of [[szaichib rikhool sacred eggs.png|the eggs]] from the individual to the tribe.  It signifies the solemn covenant, and an affirmation of the supremacy of the tribe over the individual.

Those who refuse, or are found deceitful in the *Turhool'a* suffer banishment from the tribe. 

When the eggs are ready to hatch, *ravah'ieb* begins -- the sacred struggle of the new hatchlings.  The *ravah'ieb* claims the majority of the hatchlings over the course of a ten-day.  The survivors are then full members of the tribe.

These rituals may seem savage and barbaric, but are inextricably entwined with the evolution of the [[Szaichib]].  The tribe is fundamental, and these traditions nourish a devotion to the tribe.

## First Contact
~ 2400, during the Second Wave, humans stumble onto some remote worlds of the [[Szaichib]].  Predictably, miscommunications quickly morph into violence.   

Humans were horrified and repulsed at the *ravah'ieb*, and some guileless missionaries apparently even tried to convince the aliens of their moral failing.

Mandate psitech troops devastated the Szaichib, who quickly learned to fear and hate psychics.

The szaichib are a servitor race, subjugated by an ancient race, known only as Those Who Thirst, for their penchant for "drinking" conquered worlds dry, leaving barren desertlands, for some unknown purpose.

The masters enforced strict orders and regulation on the szaichib, with swift death the punishment for trangressors. Over generations, the szaichib internalized the desire for order and unity of purpose.

Those Who Thirst promoted the differences between the tribes, and stoked the intolerance of other tribes into a rabid fanaticism.
