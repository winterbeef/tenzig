# Comealong Virus

- The virus induces temporary neurochemical changes, releasing Daxytocin to create a strong, albeit temporary, obedience and trust response towards an authority figure.
- Resistance or disobedience triggers a severe stress response, causing discomfort and anxiety.
- Over time, the human immune system begins to recognize and fight off the virus, gradually restoring normal cognitive functions.
- This process takes weeks to months, during which the infected individuals experience diminishing compliance effects.

## Symptoms and Effects

**Initial Infection:**
   - Flu-like symptoms as the body fights off the initial infection.
   - A sense of euphoria and heightened well-being in the presence of an authority figure.

**Intermediate Stage:**
   - Difficulty in independent thought and decision-making.
   - Strong aversion to actions contradicting commands, accompanied by anxiety and discomfort.

**Advanced Stage:**
   - Temporary complete compliance with authority.
   - Suppressed memories of life before infection, creating a state of temporary amnesia.
   - After a few weeks or months, the immune system begins to fight off the virus, and normal cognitive functions are gradually restored.

## Long-term Consequences

- **Fatality Rate:**
  - The infection is often fatal due to the severe strain it places on the body, particularly the brain and endocrine system.
  - Survivors are often left with compromised cognitive functions or chronic health issues, such as memory loss, reduced cognitive abilities, or endocrine disorders.

## Plot Integration

- **Discovery:** Players uncover the existence of the virus and the means to create it, realizing the terrifying potential of such a weapon.
- **Temporary Outbreak:** An accidental release causes temporary compliance among infected individuals, with the players racing against time to contain the outbreak before it becomes fatal.
- **Moral Dilemma:** Players encounter factions using the virus temporarily to control populations, raising ethical questions about the use of such a method even for short-term gains.
- **Cure Quest:** Developing or finding a cure becomes a central mission, with players navigating through dangerous territories and ethical quagmires to save infected individuals and prevent future use of the virus.
- **Survivors:** Players encounter survivors with long-term health issues, revealing the tragic consequences of the virus and motivating them to prevent its use.

## Adventure Hooks

1. **Research Station Disaster:**
   - Researchers inadvertently release the virus, causing temporary compliance among the staff. The players must quarantine the site and find a cure before fatalities occur.

2. **Hostile Takeover:**
   - A rival faction uses the virus to temporarily control a key political figure or military leader. The players must uncover the plot and reverse the effects before permanent damage is done.

3. **Virus Theft:**
   - A notorious smuggling ring steals samples of the virus, intending to sell it to the highest bidder. The players must track down the thieves and prevent the virus from falling into the wrong hands.

4. **Mysterious Epidemic:**
   - An outbreak of the virus occurs on a densely populated space station. The players must identify the source, contain the infection, and deal with the aftermath, including the compromised survivors.

5. **Ancient Lab Discovery:**
   - While exploring [[Douglass 8]], players discover an ancient lab with detailed records on the virus and its original creators. The knowledge could lead to a cure or, in the wrong hands, a powerful weapon.
