# Background

![[lassell-7-1.jpg]]

[[Lassell 7]] is a notorious space station orbiting [[Thora]], operating as a secretive black market for the galactic elite. 

Its corridors are a mix of sleek high-tech luxury and grimy, clandestine operations. The deranged yet brilliant scientist *[[Doctor Xydros Lemn]]* makes his home here. It is a dangerous haven for high-profile criminals, rogue scientists, and wealthy aristocrats who seek goods, technologies and services too dangerous normal markets.

# Loot

## Concert Cube:  The Last Concert of Konstantin Ortov

This cube projects a holographic recreation of Ortov's last concert, playing the Doulan cello at Oksana Hall, year 2660, [[Panidoulo]].  The finale is of Ortov's untitled sonata, rumored to be so beautiful as to require a saving throw.

The activation stud is along the upper side. The embedded expert system adapts the performance to the cube's surroundings, causing Ortov to appear to actually be physically present and interacting with the environment and audience, albeit usually in “canned’ ways. 

The performance can usually be heard up to 50 meters away, and the cubes will only shut down if at least 80% of the “audience” asks for them to end their set. The cubes can play for up to eight hours before requiring a recharge off a Type A cell.
## Von Neumann Swarm: *Typhon*

An extremely dangerous and illegal item.  The .25 meter grayish-silver rod is marked only with the word TYPHON, scribed into one end of the cylinder in the [[Galeirc]]an variant of standard Mandate.  No other markings are present on the rod.  There is no obvious way to open or otherwise "activate" the rod -- but why would any sane person want to?

Legend has it that a fledgling empire born of the ashes of the Silence pursued devices of maltech with ambitions of dominating their neighbors. The rumors are varied and often contradictory. Manys speak of a paranoid and unbraked AI subtly engineering its unwitting human servants to create these horrific devices. 

# Station Layout

1. **The Glass Labyrinth**: A central zone of Lassel 7, this area is a sprawling, transparent structure filled with semi-visible rooms and observation platforms. Xydros uses it for live experiments, with unsuspecting guests sometimes pulled into deadly trials.
2. **Black Needle Market**: A high-class trading zone, hidden from prying eyes. Here, elites exchange advanced tech, bio-mods, rare narcotics, and outlawed AI systems. Only those who can pay exorbitant fees in hard-to-trace currencies are allowed entry.
3. **The Alteration Wards**: Once a medical wing, now a place where those seeking extreme body modifications, illegal cybernetics, or biological enhancements come to trade their wealth for radical changes. Few leave without permanent scars, mental or physical.
4. **Q-Vault**: This section stores some of the most dangerous, experimental technologies in the galaxy—alien artifacts, time-locked devices, AI cores deemed illegal, all of which are coveted by thieves and buyers alike.
5. **The Sunless Gardens**: An eerie and beautiful zone where plants genetically altered by Dr. Xydros thrive without sunlight. Elite guests come to experience strange hallucinogenic spores or purchase bio-luminescent flora with undiscovered properties.

# Black Market Deals

1. **Illegal Weaponry**: Prototypes of weapons designed for planetary sieges, including gravitic disruptors and antimatter munitions, are for sale but only for trusted buyers.
2. **Cloning Services**: [[Doctor Xydros Lemn]] secretly clones wealthy individuals or their enemies. Some patrons seek to live forever through a series of carefully guarded clones, but the process isn’t without risks—mental degradation and identity crises are common.
3. **Exotic Biological Samples**: Rare alien DNA, harvested from extinct species, for the creation of custom organisms or genetic upgrades.
4. **Blacklisted AI Constructs**: Rogue AIs banned by most galactic authorities can be bought for a premium, each hiding dangerous sentience and algorithms that may unlock unprecedented computing power—or rebellion.
5. **Time-Distortion Drugs**: A new, experimental drug that stretches time perception, allowing users to experience hours in mere moments—perfect for those looking to escape boredom or achieve instant, focused learning, at a cost to their sanity.

# Adventure Hooks

1. **High-Stakes Auction**: A priceless alien artifact that could shift the balance of power in the sector is being auctioned off in the Black Needle Market. The PCs must infiltrate and either acquire or sabotage the auction, but every major faction in the sector is vying for it.
2. **The Fractured Mind of Xydros**: Dr. Xydros has created a dangerous AI with part of his consciousness stored within it. The AI has begun rebelling, seeking to eliminate Xydros, but also threatening the lives of everyone aboard. The PCs must either work with Xydros to shut it down or help the AI escape while navigating a treacherous mental labyrinth of Xydros' making.
3. **Bioweapon Unleashed**: One of Xydros’ experimental viruses has accidentally been released into the station's ventilation system, turning elites into violent mutants. The PCs must find a cure or quarantine the infected, all while avoiding the attention of the deranged scientist who views this chaos as "progress."
4. **Rogue Clones**: A high-ranking official’s clone has escaped the alteration wards and is hiding within the depths of Lassel 7. The PCs are tasked with capturing or rescuing it, but the clone holds memories of an intergalactic conspiracy and doesn’t want to return.
5. **Fractured Factions**: The elite clientele is starting to fracture into rival groups—some worshiping Xydros as a messianic figure, others plotting his demise. The PCs can play the factions against each other, securing valuable black market resources and technology for themselves.
