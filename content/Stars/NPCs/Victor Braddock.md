A ruthless  enforcer sent by [[Pavel Bobrikav]] to bring [[The Fuel Hole]] under corporate control. Victor is a tall and imposing figure, always impeccably dressed in a sleek  suit and fedora. He employs a network of spies and informants to gather information and eliminate any threats to the corporation's interests.

> [!quote]- The Job
> In the dimly lit corridor of The Fuel Hole, Victor Braddock, the calculating envoy of[[Novastar Energies]], regarded Olivia with a measured intensity. His cybernetic hand flexed slightly as he began, his words precise and controlled.
> 
> "You're wondering why Novastar Energies needs you," Victor said, his tone implying there was more to be said. He paused, inviting Olivia to inquire further.
> 
> Olivia met his gaze, her eyes probing. "Yes, I am. What could a group of outsiders like us possibly offer Novastar?"
> 
> Victor's lips curved into a half-smile. "Ah, perceptive. You see, the solidarity within The Gas Council runs admirably deep -- but it is not insurmountable. Novastar has a plan, a delicate dance of manipulation and subterfuge. We need individuals who can navigate this labyrinth of loyalties and ambitions without leaving a trace."
> 
> He continued, his words measured. "Your role, should you accept it, is crucial. You will be our eyes and ears, observing the Council members, deciphering their unspoken alliances, and discerning the currents beneath their words. Information is power, and your insights will guide our strategy."
> 
> Olivia nodded, absorbing his words. "But why us? There must be others more experienced, more familiar with the politics of The Fuel Hole."
> 
> Victor's artificial hand gestured slightly, emphasizing his point. "True, there are those with more experience, but they lack your independence. You are outsiders, free from the entanglements of local alliances and rivalries. Your actions won't immediately raise suspicions, allowing you to operate in the shadows, unnoticed."
> 
> He paused, allowing the implications to settle before he continued. "Moreover, you have a reputation for resourcefulness. Novastar Energies values resourceful individuals. You've survived in the harsh realities of space, adapted, and overcome. Those are precisely the qualities we need."
> 
> Olivia's expression remained thoughtful. "And what about our safety? If we're caught in the middle of this political web..."
> 
> Victor Braddock's eyes gleamed with a steely resolve. "Safety is a luxury we cannot guarantee," he said, his voice low and deliberate. "Your strength lies in your expendability, your ability to navigate the shadows unnoticed. Novastar Energies values discretion and plausible deniability."
> 
> He leaned in, his words carrying the weight of harsh reality. "You're not just pawns; you're the sharp edge of our strategy. In the volatile game we're playing, sacrifices are inevitable. Your role demands resilience, adaptability, and the understanding that the line between success and failure is razor-thin."
> 
> Olivia met his gaze, her eyes narrowing slightly. "So, we're just disposable assets to Novastar?"
> 
> Victor's expression remained unchanged, his tone unapologetic. "In this business, everyone is expendable to some extent. Novastar's investment in you is an opportunity, a chance to prove your worth. If you succeed, you'll find Novastar to be a generous patron. Fail, and the consequences will be yours to bear."
> 
> He paused, letting the weight of his words sink in before he continued. "Your autonomy, however, remains intact. We won't micromanage your actions. Consider this an alliance of convenience. Novastar provides you with resources, information, and a network, but the risks you take are yours to manage."
> 
> A flicker of uncertainty crossed Olivia's face, but she nodded, acknowledging the terms. "And if we accept, what's the first step?"
> 
> Victor's lips curved into a faint smile, acknowledging her resilience. "The first step is information. You'll receive a data package outlining your initial targets, key Gas Council members, their allegiances, and their vulnerabilities. Knowledge is power, Olivia, and it will be your most potent weapon in this endeavor."
> 
> He straightened, his posture exuding confidence. "When you're ready to proceed, contact the secure channel provided in the package. From that point on... well, let's just say you'll be in the game."
> 
> The corridor seemed to narrow as the weight of their arrangement settled upon them. Olivia's expression hardened, determination flickering in her eyes. "We'll need that information. We're not ones to back down from a challenge."
> 
> Victor nodded, acknowledging her resolve. "Good. Novastar expects results. Make the most of this opportunity, and you might find yourselves well-rewarded."
> 
> With that, he turned on his heel, his sleek form disappearing into the shadows of the corridor, leaving Olivia to contemplate the path she was about to tread.
