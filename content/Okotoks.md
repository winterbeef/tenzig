# Info, Rumors

Okotoks is a base in the belt, [[908 Porth]]. It sits in relative safety, trades minerals with [[Lycos]] and the [[Heirs of Carderas]] on [[Cost 7]].

The prospectors are about 30, and they defer to [[Lydia Roberts]] as their defacto leader

- "[[Voidsquids]]" have taken a few miners who have dug too far in search of [[cherambilth]]
- Artificial caverns have been discovered on some of the larger rocks
- The [[Ruins of Porth]] dot the rocks near [[Okotoks]].

A curio in the posession of [[Lydia Roberts]] may be the data shard that [[Danesh Ashari]] requires. 

Perhaps a deal could be made for clearing out the [[Voidsquids]] from the nearby rock that is rich with [[cherambilth]].

# Maps



### **1. The Airlock Bay**

**Purpose:** Primary entry point, where ships dock and cargo is offloaded.  
**Features:**

- A reinforced bulkhead with a jury-rigged pressure-seal system.
- Cargo crates stacked against the walls, filled with raw ore and trade goods.
- A security checkpoint manned by miners with slug-throwers, wary of pirate visitors.

2 miners with slugthrowers. Parveen Tabari, Idowu Agbaje.

> [!PDF|red] [[Stars Without Number - Rulebook.pdf#page=117&selection=67,0,109,13&color=red|Stars Without Number - Rulebook, p.113]]
> > Any scan of a ship within combat range will reveal the ship’s hull type, obvious weaponry and damage, and registered ID code.
> > 
> > Comms officers can attempt to interrogate the scans, asking one question before rolling an Int/Program skill check against difficulty 8. On a success, the question is answered, while a failure means that the data is inconclusive and no further questions can be asked of it that day. 
> > 
> > Each question after the first success that same day is asked at a cumulative +1 difficulty modifier. Questions should be simple and direct. When scanning a ship, a comms officer might ask whether particular fittings or weapons have been mounted, where good boarding points might be on the hull, whether the ship looks to be the product of a particular world or culture, or simply to question whether anything looks odd about the ship’s readings. The sensors can probe a meter or so beneath the hull skin, but they can’t intrude on a ship’s computer network or provide a map of interior spaces.

**Purpose:** Command center for the miners and deal-making space.  
**Features:**

- A simple desk with a digital ledger tracking trade and disputes.
- A cracked leather chair that’s seen better days.
- A side table with a bottle of well-aged whiskey, reserved for important negotiations.

### **3. The Smelter & Refinery**

**Purpose:** Processing raw ore into valuable trade goods.  
**Features:**

- Large, outdated industrial machinery chugging along.
- A heat haze from molten metal and a pungent scent of scorched rock.
- Stacks of refined ingots, each marked for different buyers—including the pirates.


---

### **04. The Communications Hub**

**Purpose:** Okotoks' main connection to the outside world.  
**Features:**

- A battered but functional comms array.
- Multiple encrypted channels—some clearly reserved for pirate dealings.
- A locked console with messages that could reveal **hidden betrayals**.

---

### **05. The Sickbay**

**Purpose:** Medical station for miners and wounded pirates.  
**Features:**

- A cramped but functional medbay with autodoc units and a grizzled medic.
- A bloodstained operating table with an old pirate injury chart.
- A stash of painkillers, sometimes traded at a premium.


---

### **6. The Workshop**

**Purpose:** Repair bay for mining equipment and pirate vessels.  
**Features:**

- A mishmash of industrial tools, spare hull plating, and custom-built modifications.
- A half-disassembled voidsuit with pirate insignia on the shoulder.
- A well-worn sign: “**You break it, you fix it. No exceptions.**”


---

### **9. Docks**

**Purpose:** The largest ongoing mining operation, a massive drill site.  
**Features:**

- An open mining pit with scaffolding and zero-G lifts.
- Distant echoes of picks and drills against asteroid rock.
- A set of deep tunnels rumored to hold something **not entirely natural**.

---


### **10. The Mess Hall**

**Purpose:** Communal eating area and social hub.  
**Features:**

- A long, metal-plated room with old shipboard tables and benches.
- Holo-projectors displaying asteroid belt mining reports and incoming pirate ships.
- A handwritten sign reading: “**No fights on Lydia’s watch. Take it outside.**”


---

### **11. The Barracks**

**Purpose:** Sleeping quarters for the miners.  
**Features:**

- Metal-frame bunks stacked against the walls.
- Lockers with personal effects—family photos, trinkets, old books.
- A small hydroponic garden growing vegetables under dim lights.

---

### **12. Shrine**

**Purpose:** A designated space for the **Heirs of Carderas** to rest and negotiate.  
**Features:**

- Small shrine with coins, incense, notes, pictures [[Sunita]] Guardian of Miners
- A bar with cheap liquor and a stash of contraband.
- A pirate “bulletin board” of bounties, ship sales, and opportunities.

---

### **11. The Hidden Tunnel**

**Purpose:** A secret passage leading out of Okotoks, unknown to most.  
**Features:**

- A narrow, low-gravity tunnel concealed behind storage crates.
- Scratched symbols on the walls, possibly pirate waypoints.
- Faint airflow—suggesting **it leads somewhere important**.


---

### **13. The Pirate Hold**

**Purpose:** The Heirs of Carderas’ secure storage.  
**Features:**

- A reinforced cargo bay with weapons, stolen tech, and trade goods.
- Crates labeled with pirate insignia, some still dripping with stolen blood.
- A mechanical lock with a biometric scanner keyed to pirate captains.
