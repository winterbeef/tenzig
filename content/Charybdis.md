In the real world, there is a paradox known as the "black hole information paradox," which arises from the conflict between quantum mechanics and general relativity. According to quantum mechanics, information should never be lost; however, if an object falls into a black hole, it seemingly disappears from our observable universe.

Various solutions to this paradox have been proposed, such as the "information is not lost" hypothesis, which suggests that information is encoded on the event horizon or in the Hawking radiation emitted by black holes. These ideas are still theoretical and the subject of ongoing research.
