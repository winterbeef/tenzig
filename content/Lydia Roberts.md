## **Lydia Roberts – The Anti-Corporate Powerhouse of Okotoks**

**Age:** Late 20s  
**Appearance:** Lean and wiry, her body shaped by a lifetime of hard labor in low gravity. **Short-cropped silver hair** perpetually looks like she just ran a hand through it, and her **artificial amber eyes** gleam like molten gold in dim light—an unwanted reminder of her past. She wears a reinforced work jacket over her pressure suit, stitched and patched so many times it’s practically custom armor.

**Demeanor:** **Sharp, uncompromising, and fiercely independent.** Lydia walks and talks like she has nothing to prove—because she doesn’t.

### **Background**

Lydia **hates** corporations.

Not in the vague, "I-don’t-trust-them" way that most independent miners do, but with a **deep, visceral loathing** born from firsthand experience. She grew up on a corporate mining station, watching her parents and everyone around her **squeezed dry** by contract loopholes, rigged quotas, and brutal efficiency cuts. When the company abandoned the station after an unprofitable quarter, leaving the workers to **die in vacuum**, she swore she’d **never** let anyone own her again.

She survived, but she never forgave.

Lydia made her way to Okotoks—one of the last **truly independent** mining outposts—where she worked her way up from nothing. By her early 20s, she had **outmaneuvered the old leadership**, securing trade deals with the Heirs of Carderas and making sure **no corporate flag would ever fly over Okotoks**. The miners follow her because she **fights for them**—and because, unlike a corp executive, she’ll **die before she sells them out**.

### **Personality & Leadership Style**

- **Anti-corporate to the core.** She doesn’t negotiate with corps. She undermines them, sabotages them, and if she has to, **destroys them**.
- **Tactical and calculating.** She’s not reckless—she **thinks** before she strikes. If Lydia moves against someone, it’s because she’s **already won**.
- **No tolerance for traitors.** A miner caught selling out to a corp doesn’t get a second chance. If they’re lucky, they get **exiled**. If they’re not… well, space is a big place.
- **Trusts actions, not words.** The only way to get on Lydia’s good side is to **prove yourself**. Loyalty, usefulness, and independence matter. Everything else is noise.

### **Relationships & Conflicts**

- **The Heirs of Carderas (Pirates):** Lydia has a **tense but functional truce** with Captain Rafael "Rafa" Carderas. The pirates need refined metals. The miners need supplies. But Lydia keeps them at arm’s length—she refuses to **replace** corporate overlords with pirate ones.
- **Mining Corporations:** If a corp tries to make a claim on Okotoks, Lydia will **burn their assets, hijack their shipments, and turn their own people against them** before she lets them take a single rock. She’s already done it _twice_.
- **The Miners:** They don’t always agree with her, but they respect her. Lydia has **kept them independent**, and that’s worth more than friendship.
- **Newcomers (PCs):** If the PCs show up in Okotoks and **work for a corp**, they’ll be lucky to leave with their boots. If they have **useful skills, good intel, or leverage against her enemies**, she’ll listen. If they **actively fight against the corps**, she might even trust them.

### **Adventure Hooks Involving Lydia**

1. **Corporate Incursion:** A corp-backed mercenary team is **trying to infiltrate Okotoks**, posing as independent traders. Lydia needs the PCs to **identify and eliminate them** before they establish a foothold.
2. **The Sabotage Play:** A corp has set up an **automated mining rig** on a nearby asteroid, threatening Okotoks’ market share. Lydia hires the PCs to **cripple it**—quietly or violently, their choice.
3. **The Heirs Push Too Hard:** A faction of the Heirs is **demanding more than their fair share** of the trade deal. Lydia needs muscle to **negotiate**—or remind them that Okotoks **isn’t easy prey**.
4. **A Ghost from the Past:** Someone from Lydia’s past—a former corporate executive—**wants to meet**. They claim they have **something she needs to see**. She doesn’t trust them, but she won’t risk going alone.
5. **War for the Belt:** A corp is planning a **full-scale takeover of the belt**, starting with Okotoks. Lydia needs allies, weapons, and a **preemptive strike plan**—and she needs it fast.

Lydia is a **leader forged by survival, betrayal, and defiance**. If the PCs fight alongside her, they’ll have a powerful ally—one who _never_ gives up. If they stand against her, they’ll learn firsthand why **Okotoks has never fallen.**
