# Intro

![[inuar.jpg|200]]


Long-extinct(?) synthetics.

Madness:
Rule: The world must be rectified beneath their guidance, even if they guide it only to stand still.

A reasoned madness, may be tempered when useful; and ideological, a social philosophy.


Purpose: Experimental Subjects

> [!PDF|yellow] [[Dead Names.pdf#page=21&selection=49,21,59,51&color=yellow|Dead Names, p.21]]
> >  Human life is precious. Artificial existence is less so. The creators of these synthetics wished to use them for various experimental purposes, so as to preserve their own numbers from the ravages of their investigations. Those synthetics who have survived such use are often less than gracious towards other forms of organic life, or else are so utterly enthralled by their design that they can do nothing but create the environment for their intended experiment again and again.
